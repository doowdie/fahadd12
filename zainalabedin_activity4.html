History

1989–1991: Inception and Initial EPs In the illustrious year of 1989, within the charming locale of Reading, England, Slowdive emerged, a melodic creation masterminded by the talents of Neil Halstead and Rachel Goswell in the month of October. Their harmonious journey commenced at a youthful Sunday gathering, where the duo, bonded by a friendship spanning back to their tender age of six, forayed into the realm of music as a charming indie pop ensemble christened the Pumpkin Fairies. Accompanying them were bassist Mike Cottle and percussionist Adrian Sell. Post the dissolution of the Pumpkin Fairies, Slowdive took shape, once again enlisting the rhythmic prowess of Sell alongside his acquaintance, bassist Nick Chaplin. The formation was completed with the addition of a third string virtuoso, Christian Savill, formerly affiliated with the band Eternal, who astutely responded to an advertisement seeking a female guitarist, notably offering to don a dress to secure his place. This whimsical endeavor led to his eventual recruitment. The appellation "Slowdive" sprung forth from a vivid dream experienced by Chaplin, coupled with an enlightening dialogue with Goswell, who alluded to "Slowdive", a notable single by her cherished band, Siouxsie and the Banshees. Ultimately, Goswell recounted her dismay at being outvoted regarding the naming decision. Swiftly, the ensemble recorded a demonstration and, mere months later, graced the stage alongside the band Five Thirty. Steve Walters, the esteemed head of A&R at EMI, was present at this performance and, captivated, sought one of their demos. Consequently, Slowdive entered into a recording contract with Creation Records shortly thereafter, with the band’s average age hovering at a tender 19. However, Sell, perceiving an overwhelming tempo in their ascendancy, opted to depart for university after a mere six months within the collective. In the illustrious month of November in the year 1990, a self-titled EP was unveiled, garnering accolades from discerning music critics. The genesis of Slowdive lay in their original demo; indeed, the band gravitated towards these earlier recordings, feeling a profound disillusionment with their studio endeavors. In a resplendent commendation, NME scribe Simon Williams proclaimed, "Slowdive have dispelled the confines that hinder creativity... When they truly succumb to relaxation, Slowdive can render the Cocteau Twins akin to Mudhoney." The esteemed Melody Maker conferred upon the EP its prestigious "Single of the Week" distinction, a merit subsequently awarded to the band’s following two EPs. Drummer Neil Carter, hailing from the fellow Reading ensemble the Colour Mary, graced the Morningrise EP, yet departed before its release in February 1991. Simon Scott assumed the drumming role following the dissolution of his prior band, the alternative rock collective known as the Charlottes. The Holding Our Breath EP emerged in June 1991, ascending to No. 52 on the UK Albums Chart, whilst the single "Catch the Breeze" achieved the pinnacle of the UK Indie Chart.

Most Popular Song

Live at Pitchfork Music Festival 2014

"When the sun hits" stands as the most emblematic and cherished piece of the entire collection, and I fully comprehend the reason for its acclaim. It was unequivocally the tender vocals and resplendent, nostalgic guitars that rendered the composition quite remarkable, yet what truly captivated my essence was the atmospheric conditions. Strolling solitary amidst a chilling, window-draped milieu under an icy sun that punctured the pale azure heavens, this melody resonated through my eardrums, sweeping away every preconceived notion I harbored regarding the essence of music. The composition illuminated the paramount element of this album, which radiated resplendently here: the exquisitely beautiful and atmospheric choruses that entwine, undulate, and swirl in one’s auditory senses long after the final note has resonated. The introductory guitar riff, echoing in my ears, sent an exquisite shiver down my spine, and as Neil’s gentle, ethereal voice ebbed in to serenade, it rendered the encounter even more poignantly bittersweet. The profound fervor of the track endures upon subsequent listens. A few months post my inaugural encounter with this tune, I resolved to embrace the entirety of the album, as it continues to be heralded as one of the most revered masterpieces throughout musical history, transcending its own genre.
